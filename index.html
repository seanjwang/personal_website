ABOUT ME

I am a Mechanical Engineering PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University. I am advised by Aaron Johnson and work in the RoboMechanics Lab. My current research is focused on using robots for environmental sampling. As a part of this research, I have been looking at using reinforcement learning for rough terrain traversal of wheeled robots. I also focus on planning sampling locations for maximal information gain.

Prior to my PhD, I was an undergradate researcher at the at University of California, Santa Barbara, working with Professor Francesco Bullo. My reserach focus was on path planning for single and multi agent surveillance UAV systems. I was awarded the "" for my undergraduate research.

During my undergrad, I was also an mechanical engineer intern at Continental and Strand Products. At Continental, my work focused on the mechanical design of long range lidar sensors for use in automated vehicles. At Strand Producst, my work focused on automating the manufacturing process of cable assemblies.
